A Human's Guide to RL Algorithms

Q-Learning

Definition:

A method that learns the value of actions in specific states by using a simple table (a "cheat sheet").

Industry Use Case:

Used in simple robotics for navigation, like optimizing the cleaning path for a robot vacuum in a home, or for basic inventory management systems to find the quickest path to an item.

Deep Q-Network (DQN)

Definition:

Uses a neural network (a 'brain') to guess the value of actions, allowing it to handle complex or unseen states.

Industry Use Case:

Used by companies like Google's DeepMind to master video games, which also applies to optimizing resource management in data centers or training ad-recommendation systems to show relevant content.

Policy Gradient Methods (e.g., REINFORCE)

Definition:

Directly learns a 'policy,' or the probability of which action to take, to get the best reward.

Industry Use Case:

Ideal for robotics where precise, continuous control is needed. For example, a factory robot arm learning the exact amount of force and angle needed to pick up and place delicate objects like computer chips.

Actor-Critic

Definition:

Uses two networks: an 'Actor' to decide on an action and a 'Critic' to judge how good that action was.

Industry Use Case:

Powers advanced robotics, such as helping bipedal robots (like those from Boston Dynamics) learn to walk, run, and keep their balance on uneven terrain, which is crucial for search-and-rescue.

Proximal Policy Optimization (PPO)

Definition:

A stable algorithm that improves its policy by taking small, safe steps, avoiding big, risky changes.

Industry Use Case:

A go-to for complex, large-scale problems. It's used to train sophisticated AI for e-sports (like Dota 2) and in finance for creating automated stock trading strategies that can adapt to market changes.
